What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

ROI answers: "Will this degree pay off financially?" We calculate over 10 years after graduation — but we also factor in school prestige, network quality, and career outcomes.

COMPOSITE SCORE (0–100)
Combines financial ROI (50%), prestige & rigor (25%), and student outcomes (25%). An Ivy League school at high cost can still outscore a cheap school because of lifetime network value and salary trajectory.
PRESTIGE FACTOR
Admission selectivity, graduation rates, real IRS-verified earnings, and employer recognition all factor in. Elite schools cost more but produce measurably different career outcomes over a lifetime.
NET GAIN
Total 10-year earnings minus all costs paid. Positive = degree paid off. We then weight by prestige because not all earnings gains come from ROI alone — your network and brand matter.
BREAK EVEN
When your cumulative earnings finally exceed all costs. Elite schools often break even later but earn significantly more per year in the 5–15 year window.

The Hidden Systems Behind Your College Bill

The decision you make at 17 runs through government algorithms, federal loan servicers, and salary data pipelines most students never see.

FAFSA ALGORITHM (SAI)
A government formula silently calculates how much your family "can" pay. Most families never learn how it works — or that you can appeal it.
COLLEGE SCORECARD
The US Dept. of Education tracks IRS-verified earnings for every school's graduates. We pull this live — it's the most accurate salary data available.
PRESTIGE NETWORK
Studies show elite school alumni networks produce 15–30% higher salaries at the 10-year mark vs. comparable students at regional schools.
BLS + NACE DATA
We blend Bureau of Labor Statistics occupational data with NACE salary surveys and school-specific Scorecard earnings for the most accurate projections.

Hidden Costs — What No Acceptance Letter Mentions

Tuition is one part of what you actually pay. Source: College Board Trends in College Pricing 2024–25.

ROOM & BOARD · $14,470/yr
On-campus housing and meal plan average $14,470/yr. Over 4 years: $57,880 — often more than in-state tuition.
BOOKS & SUPPLIES · $1,240/yr
Publishers release new editions every 3 years to kill resale value. Plan for $4,960 over 4 years.
PERSONAL EXPENSES · $2,020/yr
Clothing, laundry, toiletries — College Board estimates $2,020/yr not including entertainment.
MANDATORY FEES · $900/yr
Student activity, health center, technology fees — charged to every student whether you use them or not.
True annual cost = Tuition + roughly $21,000–$23,000/year in additional costs
