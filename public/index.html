What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

ROI measures whether your college degree pays off financially. We compare what you'll earn in 10 years against what you paid for school.

NET ROI
Total 10-year earnings minus total costs paid. Positive = your degree paid off financially.
ROI GRADE (A+ to F)
A+ means you earned 3× what you paid. F means your costs exceeded your earnings — a financial loss.
BREAK-EVEN POINT
The month post-graduation when cumulative earnings finally exceed your total education costs.
DEBT-TO-INCOME RATIO
Monthly loan payment ÷ monthly gross income. Lenders consider above 20% a financial stress indicator.

The Invisible Infrastructure Behind Your College Bill

Every college decision is processed through hidden systems that most students never learn exist — until they're paying them back.

THE FAFSA ALGORITHM
A government formula (EFC/SAI) silently calculates how much aid you qualify for. Most families don't know how it works or how to optimize it.
FEDERAL LOAN SERVICERS
Companies like Nelnet and MOHELA are assigned to collect your debt — you don't choose them. They manage a combined $1.7 trillion in student loans.
COLLEGE SCORECARD API
The US Dept. of Education tracks earnings outcomes for every college. We pull directly from this database — data most students never see before enrolling.
BLS SALARY PIPELINE
The Bureau of Labor Statistics tracks 800+ occupations. Their Occupational Employment Statistics (OES) shape salary expectations — we use the raw data directly.

Hidden Fees: What No Acceptance Letter Mentions

Tuition is just the start. Based on College Board 2023 data, here's what's hiding in your real bill:

ROOM & BOARD · $12,990/yr
On-campus housing and a meal plan. Over 4 years that's $51,960 — often MORE than in-state tuition at public universities.
BOOKS & SUPPLIES · $1,240/yr
Publishers release new editions every 3 years to prevent resale. That's $4,960 over 4 years just for reading material.
PERSONAL EXPENSES · $2,120/yr
College Board estimates $2,120/year in personal costs — clothing, toiletries, entertainment — for students living on campus.
MANDATORY FEES · $400–$800/yr
Student activity fees, health center fees, technology fees — charged to every student regardless of whether they use the services.
True annual cost = Tuition + ~$17,350–$21,150/year in additional costs · Source: College Board 2023
