What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

ROI measures whether your degree pays off financially over 10 years versus what you paid for school.

OVERALL SCORE
Our composite uses absolute real-world benchmarks — DTI (27%), ROI grade (22%), monthly payment (20%), wellness (14%), interest (10%), salary-to-debt (7%). Scores reflect actual financial health, not just which school is cheaper.
NET ROI
Total 10-year earnings minus all costs. Positive = your degree paid off. This is one factor, not the whole picture.
BREAK-EVEN AGE
The exact age when cumulative earnings finally exceed total costs paid. The sooner, the better.
DEBT-TO-INCOME
Monthly loan ÷ monthly gross income. Above 20% signals a decade of financial stress. The #1 quality-of-life killer.

The Hidden Systems Behind Your College Bill

Every college decision is processed through hidden systems most students never learn exist — until they're paying them back for a decade.

THE FAFSA ALGORITHM
A government formula (SAI) silently calculates your aid eligibility. Most families never learn how to optimize it.
FEDERAL LOAN SERVICERS
Companies like Nelnet and MOHELA are assigned to collect your debt — you don't choose them. They manage $1.7 trillion in loans.
COLLEGE SCORECARD API
The US Dept. of Education tracks earnings outcomes for every school. We pull from this database — data most students never see.
BLS SALARY PIPELINE
The Bureau of Labor Statistics tracks 800+ occupations. Their OES data shapes salary expectations — we use the raw numbers.

DATA SOURCES & METHODOLOGY
SALARY DATA
Starting salaries from NACE Salary Survey, Spring 2024 (National Association of Colleges and Employers). Growth rates from BLS Employment Cost Index 2024 and PayScale compensation research. Per-school earnings from US Dept. of Education College Scorecard 2022-23 institutional data. Blended 50/50: BLS national average + school-specific 6-year graduate outcomes.
COLLEGE COSTS
Tuition and cost of attendance from US Dept. of Education College Scorecard API (live federal data). Hidden fees from College Board Trends in College Pricing 2024-25: Room & Board $14,470 avg, Books $1,240, Personal $2,020, Transportation $1,230, Tech/Fees $900, Health Insurance $2,900.
FINANCIAL AID MODEL
Family contribution estimated using Federal Student Aid EFC Formula Guide 2024-25 simplified need analysis (studentaid.gov). Income Protection Allowances from 2024-25 federal tables. Need coverage rates from NACUBO FY2024 Study of Endowments and College Board Institutional Aid data: Elite private ~97%, Strong private ~76%, Average private ~57%, Public flagship ~35%.
LOAN CALCULATIONS
Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loan rate: 6.53% (2024-25) per studentaid.gov. Standard 10-year repayment plan. Interest accrual modeled from disbursement date — earlier loans compound longer before repayment begins. Tax rate: 25.6% effective (13% federal + 5% state avg + 7.65% FICA per Tax Foundation 2024).
COMPOSITE SCORE
Absolute scoring using real-world benchmarks (not relative normalization). DTI thresholds from CFPB Qualified Mortgage Rule. Salary-to-debt benchmarks from consumer finance research. Weights: DTI 27% · ROI Grade 22% · Monthly Payment 20% · Wellness 14% · Interest 10% · Salary-to-Debt 7%. Small relative tie-breaker (±8 pts) separates similar schools.
OPPORTUNITY COST
S&P 500 historical average: 10.5% nominal / 7.5% real (inflation-adjusted) per Damodaran (NYU Stern) historical market returns database and Vanguard 2024 Long-Term Market Outlook. Represents what tuition dollars could grow to if invested instead of spent on education.
All projections are estimates for educational guidance only. Actual aid, costs, and salaries will vary. Always verify with official FAFSA results and financial aid award letters before making enrollment decisions.
